      Vrinda N wrote a review Oct 2019
      18 contributions3 helpful votes
      Comfortable, clean, charming
      This family-run hotel In the heart of Fontainhas (the Latin Quarter) has retained its olde worlde charm over the years. The owner is invested in its day-to-day running, which perhaps makes a difference. The rooms, the antique (and well-maintained) furniture, the imaginative flooring, and the dining hall with its showcases of artefacts, all make for a delightful, laid-back experience, and when you check out they gift you small bottles of their own specially-brewed feni liqeuer. The hotel's proximity to the city centre makes it a convenient place to stay in, while a quiet ramble through the Latin Quarter can throw up amazing sights. Old-timer Rohini (Housekeeping) is dedicated to keeping your room clean and deserves a special mention. I would definitely recommend this place to anyone who wants to have a feel of Goa old and new, and who prefers tasteful comfort and cleanliness to the sterile, impersonal monotony of a 5-star chain. The Verandah (the in-house restaurant) is a good place to spend an afternoon or an evening, but the menu could do with an uplift and the staff perhaps trained to recommend what's actually fresh (some of them do, but some are apt to push you into ordering something which can be a let-down if you know your seafood!)…
